Textbook

We use the relatively new text book, “Operating System Concept Essentails (Second Edition)”, by Avi Silberschatz et al. It is based on “Operating System Concepts (Ninth Edition)” and comprises a subset of chapters of the ninth edition and do not cover all the topics in the ninth edition. You can buy it in the following links:

  • Operating System Concept Essentials 2e: ¥297
  • Operating System Concept Essentials 2e (eTextbook Digital Rental 180 days): $49.50

Grading Policy

The score of 106F14A class includes five parts: examination, experiments, homework, in-class quiz and attendance. The detailed percentage are as following.

  • 40% Examination (open-book)
  • 20% Experiments (reports)
  • 20% Homework (6)
  • 10% In-class Quiz (6)
  • 10% Attendance
Note: the final examination will cover material from lecture, in-class quizzes, experiments and homework.

Class Policy

There are several policies for our class:
  • All homework and experiments must be submitted by the due date. A 25% penalty will be applied for homework and experiment report submitted each day after the deadline;
  • Please remove headphones/earbuds in class. Hats are OK as long as they don’t cover your face. I want to communicate with you, not your mobile device;
  • Attendance is expected at every class session. Absence from class is only permissible due to a documented personal illness or similar extenuating circumstance;
  • Class periods will encompass lecture and discussion of the course material. I encourage you to ask questions in class, read the textbook, and ask your classmates;
  • If unforeseen emergencies impact the course, the course grading scheme may be modified at the instructor’s discretion.

We encourage students to talk to each other about the course. In particular, you can always discuss technical details about software used in the course. You can share design information, help each other debug, brainstorm ideas, etc. Academic integrity requires that when you submit work, you make it clear who contributed to the work and from where ideas came.
